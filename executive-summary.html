Situation

Taylors Arm is a small Nambucca Valley hinterland village. The investigation package records a flying fox colony affecting residential amenity, tank water reliance, outdoor use of homes, and the tourism precinct around the Pub With No Beer.

The colony was described in the March 2023 council record as having more than doubled in size and roosting in Camphor Laurel trees behind residential properties.

Record

On 16 March 2023, Vicki Provost addressed Nambucca Valley Council at a public meeting held at Taylors Arm Hall. The council minutes record the presentation and the request about the Camphor Laurel trees.

The investigation reviewed available council records from March 2023 to April 2026 and records a public-document response void.

Findings

The Evidence Register is frozen at EVID-076. It records the presentation, the absence from Outstanding Actions records, the Camphor Laurel pathway, the national monitoring pathway, relevant grant pathways, and the Bellbrook regional comparison.

The MAYHEM analysis layer identifies the issue as a governance architecture problem rather than an individual-intent claim.

Available pathways

The package identifies camp notification, Camphor Laurel environmental review, grant access, recipient escalation, and a cross-corridor submission pathway.

Bigger picture

The package treats Taylors Arm and Bellbrook as a regional corridor pattern under similar species, policy, geography, and representation conditions.
