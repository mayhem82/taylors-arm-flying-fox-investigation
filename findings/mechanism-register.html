SECTION ONE — CONFIRMED ACTIVE STRUCTURAL HARM MECHANISMS

Eleven structural harm mechanisms confirmed active against the frozen evidence register. Confirmation means the evidence register holds primary source documentation sufficient to establish each mechanism as operative. Mechanisms are drawn from the Structural Harm Mechanism Catalogue v2.0. All descriptions name what systems do. No intent is attributed to any actor.

Mechanism 8 — Compliant Process Harm

The council followed its procedures. The public forum was advertised. Vicki Provost registered correctly. The meeting was held. The presentation was received. The minutes were produced. Every step of the process was executed in compliance with the council's own meeting procedures. The harm continued. The process did not produce the outcome it was designed to enable — community access to governance response. Documented compliance coexisted with functional non-response.

Evidence: EVID-001 through EVID-007.

Mechanism 9 — Documented Compliance as Shield

The State of Our Valley Report 2022-2024 records the March 2023 Taylors Arm away meeting as a civic achievement. The away meetings are cited as evidence of council's community engagement. The substance of the engagement at that meeting — a formal flying fox infestation complaint — generated no institutional response. The documented process is being used, through the annual report claim, as evidence of a community focus that the record does not support. The achievement claim and the response void coexist in the same institutional record.

Evidence: EVID-029, EVID-024, EVID-028.

Mechanism 10 — Time as Structural Harm Mechanism

Minimum 37 months elapsed from the registered complaint to the most recent retrieved council agenda. The Outstanding Actions register is the mechanism designed to track unresolved matters. It contained no entry for this matter at any point in the documented period. Time has not resolved the complaint. It has erased it from institutional tracking. Per Function 8 (Temporal Inversion) from the Yield Catalogue, time loads onto the institution, not the community — the 37-month void is now the evidentiary record.

Evidence: EVID-007, EVID-008, EVID-009, EVID-026, EVID-027.

Mechanism 11 — Silence and Non-Response

No resolution, referral, action item, or staff direction was recorded following Vicki Provost's March 2023 presentation. No response to Vicki Provost appears in any public document across the 37-month window. No council staff assessment of the Camphor Laurel removal request appears in any public document. The silence began 16 March 2023. It continues in the evidence.

Evidence: EVID-007, EVID-008, EVID-009, EVID-026.

Mechanism 21 — Prevention Foreclosure

Available preventative pathways existed and were not pursued. The Camphor Laurel removal pathway required a Review of Environmental Factors — Kempsey Shire Council demonstrated this in April 2024. The camp registration pathway required notification to CSIRO or NSW Environment — the contact details are publicly available. The grant program pathway required a registered camp and an application — the program existed and had been accessed by the same council for Nambucca Heads in 2017. None of these pathways were initiated in the documented period. The prevention window has been open for a minimum of 37 months.

Evidence: EVID-013, EVID-014, EVID-021, EVID-030, EVID-059.

Mechanism 25 — Isolated Incident Framing

The Bellbrook colony and the Taylors Arm colony exist in the same regional corridor. Both have been treated by their respective councils as separate, discrete local matters. No cross-council recognition of the corridor pattern appears in any public document. The state government grant program and the national monitoring program exist precisely because grey-headed flying fox management is a regional and national pattern — not an isolated local incident. The two councils' treatment of adjacent hinterland colonies as unrelated local matters is Mechanism 25 in operation.

Evidence: EVID-012 through EVID-016, EVID-019, EVID-020.

Mechanism 41 — Legitimacy Production Decoupled from Enforcement

Nambucca Valley Council produced the following legitimacy-generating outputs during the response void: two annual reports citing community engagement achievements; the State of Our Valley Report 2022-2024; the June 2023 sporting reserve concept design process in the same precinct as the reported infestation; and a documented schedule of away meetings cited as evidence of community reach. None of these outputs engaged with the flying fox complaint. The legitimacy production and the enforcement failure are decoupled.

Evidence: EVID-023, EVID-024, EVID-025, EVID-028, EVID-029.

Mechanism 43 — Accountability Inversion

The 133-person village of Taylors Arm holds no structural standing in the mechanisms that could produce a response. The Flying-Fox Camp Management Policy 2015 empowers councils — it does not require councils to respond to community requests. The Biosecurity Act creates obligations on the council as Local Control Authority — but enforcement of those obligations requires a complaint to a body above the council, which has not been documented. The community bears the full cost of the harm. The institution bears no cost of non-response. The inversion is complete.

Evidence: EVID-010, EVID-011, EVID-070, EVID-071.

Mechanism 46 — Accountability Cost Externalisation

Vicki Provost registered, prepared, and delivered a formal presentation to council at a meeting held in the village. The council recorded the presentation and took no action. Every cost of accountability-seeking — the registration, the preparation, the presentation, the subsequent absence of response — was borne by Vicki Provost and the Taylors Arm community. The institution bore no cost. The complaint suppression pattern documented in the DFAPTA — no subsequent public forum registration after March 2023 — is structurally produced by this mechanism: the cost of accountability-seeking is non-zero and the return has been zero across 37 months.

Evidence: EVID-006, EVID-007, EVID-008.

Mechanism 48 — Complexity as Barrier

The dual-jurisdiction compliance framework — NSW Biosecurity Act, Biodiversity Conservation Act, EPBC Act — intersecting with the Flying-Fox Camp Management Policy 2015 and the Flying-Fox Camp Management Code of Practice 2018 — constitutes a governance instrument architecture of sufficient density that no community member without specialist knowledge could independently navigate it to identify available pathways. The community's accessible ask — remove the Camphor Laurel trees — was the instinctive reduction of this complexity to its most navigable component. The council's non-response left the community without the institutional guidance that would convert the complexity into a navigable pathway.

Evidence: EVID-010, EVID-022, EVID-068, EVID-069, EVID-070, EVID-072.

Mechanism 54 — Reform as Reset

The mayoral transition of October 2024 has functioned structurally as a reset. The incoming council under Mayor Gary Lee has no institutional record of the March 2023 complaint because it was never entered into the Outstanding Actions register. The community's complaint history did not transfer. Any future advocacy begins from zero. The mechanism operated through administrative silence, not intent.

Evidence: EVID-007, EVID-008, EVID-018.

Mechanism 56 — Language Barrier as Structural Exclusion

The National Flying-Fox Monitoring Program, the Flying-Fox Camp Management Policy 2015, the Flying-Fox Camp Management Code of Practice 2018, the Biosecurity Act 2015 obligations for local control authorities, and the NSW WeedWise priority weed designation framework are all published in institutional register language that is formally accessible but functionally illegible to a community member without specialist knowledge. No obligation exists on Nambucca Valley Council to translate any of these instruments into plain terms for the Taylors Arm community. The community's access to remedy is structurally gated behind institutional register language that the community has no documented access to.

Evidence: EVID-010, EVID-011, EVID-012, EVID-030, EVID-070, EVID-071.

Mechanism 60 — Normalisation Through Duration

The Bellbrook colony has been active since November 2019. The Taylors Arm colony was described as having more than doubled by March 2023, implying prior presence. The absence of any management plan, any monitoring registration, any council response, and any electoral advocacy across these periods creates the structural conditions for normalisation. The colony is no longer an emergency or an incursion — it is the operating baseline against which any future response will be measured. The failed state risks becoming the calibrated normal.

Evidence: EVID-016, EVID-006, EVID-008, EVID-009.
